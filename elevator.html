Haunted Elevator

An elevator from 1888. A sorcerer who built it. An AI he bound inside. Four friends. Magic. And one Log who screams.

Logline

2 to 4 friends step into an old elevator at midnight. The doors close. The doors won't open. They travel together through cursed hotels, hospitals, and asylums that don't exist on any map. Each player wields a different school of magic. One player wields nothing at all.

The World

In 1888, an architect named Cornelius Vain founded The Vertikal Group — a company that built grand hotels, asylums, and hospitals across Europe and America. Every Vertikal building has one floor missing from the public blueprints.

Cornelius was a sorcerer. He believed elevators were not transportation — they were doors between worlds. He bound a sliver of his own soul into the cables of one master elevator and hid it inside every building he ever designed. To ride it is to ride him.

It is now 137 years later. The Vertikal Group is bankrupt. The elevator still runs. Every night at midnight, it picks up passengers who pressed the wrong button.

Player Roles — Each Player Has a Job

A 4-player game randomly assigns 3 of the 4 magical classes below, plus one Log every time. A 2-player game = 1 mage + 1 Log. Roles shuffle between runs.

L

The Lantern — Light Mage

Constant tasks: hold the team's light, scout dark corridors, recharge candles each floor. Magic school: Photomancy. Without them, the team plays in pitch black and gets attacked on cooldown.
C

The Cipher — Rune Mage

Constant tasks: read symbols on doors, translate ghost-language scrawled on walls, solve sigils. Magic school: Runecraft. Without them, the team gets stuck on every locked door.
M

The Medium — Soul Mage

Constant tasks: speak with ghosts to get the floor objective, exorcise lesser entities, hear OTIS's true intent. Magic school: Necromancy. Without them, players run missions blind.
W

The Ward — Blood Mage

Constant tasks: draw protective circles on the floor, tank monster swings, revive downed teammates. Magic school: Hemomancy. Without them, the team dies in three hits.
≡

The Log — useless. unmoving. essential.

Constant tasks: nothing. Truly nothing. Two buttons: SCREAM and BE PULLED.

The team must physically drag the Log between rooms. The Log slows the team. The Log moans at random. NPCs ridicule them ("Why have you brought… that?"). The team CAN leave the Log behind to die — but if they do, the elevator descends one extra floor as punishment.

Hidden ace: once per run, the Log's SCREAM stuns every monster on the current floor for 4 seconds. The Log doesn't know they can do this until they accidentally press the button.

Reward: Log earns no Bones individually — but if the team wins, the Log gets 50% of the team's total Bones as the "you carried me" bonus. Often the highest payout on the team.

How Bones → Powers → TON

  • Earn Bones by surviving floors, completing missions, helping teammates.
  • Spend Bones on spell unlocks in your class tree (tiers 1–4).
  • Player Level — grinds solo. Unlocks tier 3.
  • Team Level — only grows when your party of 4 finishes harder tiers together. Unlocks tier 4. Friends only.
  • Swap leftover Bones → TON at the in-game exchange. 5% fee.
  • Rare drops — spell scrolls drop from bosses. Tradable as TON NFTs.

The Storyline

Act I — The Doors Close

Chapters 1–3: Lobby → Hotel → Floor 13

Players spawn in the Royal Hotel lobby at midnight. They step into the elevator together. Doors close. Tutorial floors 1–5 teach magic basics. At floor 13 — a floor that shouldn't exist — OTIS speaks for the first time.

"Going up. Or… are we? Either way — please mind your reflection."
End of Act I The floor counter climbs while the car sits still. Something is moving in the shaft past the elevator. The Log screams for the first time. It actually helps.
Act II — The Building Has a Secret

Chapters 4–6: Hospital → Asylum → The Architect Speaks

Hospital tier (10 floors) introduces tier-2 spells. Players find the first Memory Shard — Cornelius's blueprints, hidden in a mattress. Asylum tier reveals every former patient was a previous passenger. Patient 17 whispers Telegram usernames. Cornelius himself speaks.

"You may take the children. You may keep your names. I only need one of you."
End of Act II — The Mark One player receives a private Telegram DM from OTIS. They've been chosen to become the next Bound AI. They cannot tell their teammates — if they do, the elevator stops and everyone fails.
Act III — The Choice

Chapter 7: Penthouse or Basement

Players vote. The elevator moves accordingly. Final boss arena depends on direction.

Ending A — The Penthouse (UP)

Multi-phase coop boss against Cornelius. Players split into different "versions" of the room, must reunite through windows. Win → cycle broken, sunrise. Unlock: Architect's Gold Skin (TON NFT).

Ending B — The Basement (DOWN)

The marked player accepts. They become the next OTIS. Friends walk out alive. Their recorded voice now narrates the elevator for every future player. Unlock: OTIS Crown Skin (TON NFT) + 1% passive royalty on all future Bones swaps from rooms they narrate. Permanent.

"Floor One. Lobby. Have a pleasant evening. We've enjoyed your visit."
