Logline

Last train. Midnight. 4 to 6 passengers. The PA crackles three stations in: "One of you isn't getting off." One player is secretly possessed — even they don't know yet. Survive to the terminal. Vote right. Or ride forever.

The World

Beneath every great city runs The Underline — a parallel rail network using real stations, real platforms, real schedules. Trains that run after midnight don't appear on any timetable. They pick up the lost, the late, the half-dead, and the merely cursed.

The Underline is run by The Conductor — a sorcerer-priest from 1944 bound to a PA system. He doesn't know he's dead. The trains run because he believes they should.

Most passengers ride one stop and exit at dawn, none the wiser. But sometimes a train picks up a soul that doesn't belong. Then the doors stop opening. Then the lights start to flicker. Then the missions begin.

The Storyline

Act I — Last Train

Chapters 1–3: Boarding → Tutorial Stations → The Announcement

Players spawn on an empty platform. The destination board flickers between languages. A train pulls in with the doors already open. The PA welcomes them by name. Stations 1 and 2 teach mechanics. At Station 3 the PA goes silent for 7 seconds, then The Conductor speaks. One random player gets a private Telegram DM.

"One of you isn't getting off. The rest of you will need to decide who."
End of Act I — The Knowledge The Possessed now knows. Their friends don't. Voice chat becomes paranoia chat. Every shadow becomes evidence. Every silence becomes a confession.
Act II — The Lines Bleed

Chapters 4–6: Wrong Tunnel → Transfer Tickets → Babushka Speaks

The train takes a curve no rail map shows. The next station is bilingual — Russian on top, Japanese on bottom. Players find paper tickets from 1944, 1962, 1985, 2001 — each ticket plays a 10-second cutscene revealing a previous casualty. If players share food with Babushka three times, she finally talks.

"He gets off today. One of you will take his place."
End of Act II — The Salaryman Wakes The Salaryman finally looks up from his newspaper. He realizes. He screams. Temporary ally — but only until the next station.
Act III — Terminal Station

Chapter 7: The Final Vote

Train pulls into the last station. 60 seconds to dawn. Doors will only open if surviving players correctly identify The Possessed. Each player gets one vote. Tie = doors stay shut.

Ending A — Daylight

Right vote. Doors open. Sun floods in. The Possessed is dragged off the train by hands you cannot see. Survivors step onto a real platform in a real city, alive, paid out in TON. Unlock: Sunrise Ticket NFT.

Ending B — The Hell Line

Wrong vote. Train enters a tunnel that wasn't there a moment ago. All players become permanent passengers of the nightmare tier. Compensation: Damned Soul cosmetic, a free pass into the next round, and the Possessed gets 3 free Possessed games (huge advantage).

"Terminal station. End of service. Thank you for riding with us. We've enjoyed your company."
